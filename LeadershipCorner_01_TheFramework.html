The Framework
Two Things Every Sales Leader Does
Lead by Example
Field time is valuable and always welcome, but it is not the requirement. What is required is that your own standards, your consistency, and your follow-through are something a Pilot would want to model. They notice whether you show up when you say you will.
In Practice Show up to every scheduled check-in, on time, prepared, every time.
Hold Them Accountable
Encouragement without accountability creates comfortable underperformers. A good leader sets a clear target, checks it on a real cadence, and has honest conversations when the activity or the results aren't there. Softening the truth to spare feelings, what's sometimes called ruinous empathy, isn't kindness. It just delays the conversation.
In Practice Recurring check-ins that look at real numbers and real curriculum progress, not just vibes.
