Standing Reference
Accountability Checklist
Use this at any point in a Pilot's career, not just during onboarding.
You've checked in with this Pilot on a real, scheduled cadence, not just in passing.
Your last check-in covered real numbers and real progress, not just how they're feeling about the job.
If this is a Rookie Pilot, curriculum, assignments, and Learning Lift-Off attendance are on track for the four-month deadline.
If activity or results have slipped, there's a specific plan attached to it, with a date to revisit.
This Pilot knows exactly what's expected of them right now, not just when they started.
You've told them, specifically, something they did well recently.
