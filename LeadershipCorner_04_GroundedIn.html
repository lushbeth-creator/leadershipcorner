Where This Approach Comes From
Leadership Corner borrows from well-established schools of thought on developing people. Click a card to flip it.
Coaching, Not Just Managing
Ask Before You Tell
Click to reveal
Training builds skill. Coaching builds ownership. When a Pilot is stuck, resist the urge to hand them the answer. Ask a question that gets them to find it themselves, and protect real, dedicated check-in time that isn't just a status update.
Inspired by Keith Rosen's sales coaching methodology
Leadership as Influence
Consistency Over Intensity
Click to reveal
Leadership is built through small, repeated actions, not one big effort. A leader's ceiling determines their team's ceiling, and the leaders who go furthest are the ones who shift from just producing results to developing other people who can produce results too.
Inspired by John C. Maxwell's leadership principles
Growth Doesn't Just Happen
Be Intentional About Their Growth
Click to reveal
A Pilot doesn't grow just by putting in time in the seat. Growth happens when a leader plans it on purpose: a specific skill to build next, a specific conversation to have, a specific date to check the progress. Without a plan, experience alone rarely turns into improvement.
Inspired by John Maxwell's Law of Intentionality
Care Personally, Challenge Directly
Practice Radical Candor
Click to reveal
Softening a real problem to spare someone's feelings in the moment feels kind, but it leaves them without what they actually need to improve. The most respectful thing you can do is say the hard thing directly, while making it obvious you're invested in them getting better.
Inspired by Kim Scott's Radical Candor framework
Play the Infinite Game
There's No Final Score
Click to reveal
Leading a sales team isn't a game you win once and walk away from. The leaders who last stop chasing a single quarter and instead build trust and safety that outlasts it. A leader's job isn't to personally deliver the numbers, it's to develop the people who do.
Inspired by Simon Sinek's The Infinite Game and Leaders Eat Last
Lead With Emotional Intelligence
Patience Is a Leadership Skill
Click to reveal
Patience, empathy, and self-awareness aren't soft extras, they're what separates a leader who builds a lasting team from one who only chases this month's number. A parent invests in someone's whole development. A babysitter just watches to make sure nothing goes wrong.
Inspired by Gary Vaynerchuk's leadership philosophy
Leadership Principle
When a Pilot's performance slips, the first question is never "what's wrong with them." It's "when did I last check in, and was that conversation honest." Most performance problems are leadership problems in disguise, often because a leader cared enough to stay quiet instead of caring enough to say the hard thing.
